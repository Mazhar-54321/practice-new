````
## 1. Simple Present

**Used for:** routine, schedules, general truths and facts

````

I / You / We / They + verb
He / She / It + verb + s/es

```

**My sentences:**
1. I work from home
2. I eat dates and figs every morning
3. I drop my wife off at Afzal Gunj every morning
4. The sun rises in the east
5. The flight arrives at 8:00 AM

**Note:** Schedule means officially fixed — by airline, manager, or organisation. Routine means personally fixed — habit or team pattern. Both use Simple Present.

**Takeaway:** Simple Present = routine + schedules + general truths and facts

---

## 2. Present Continuous

**Used for:** happening now, temporary situations, future planned (100% it will be executed)

```

I + am + verb(ing)
He / She / It + is + verb(ing)
We / You / They + are + verb(ing)

```

**My sentences:**
1. I am working on Payload CMS — happening now
2. I am working with a contractor for 6 months — temporary
3. I am visiting Bangalore next week — future arrangement
4. I am using Infinix 30 Pro until I get Google Pixel — temporary
5. I am meeting her in the evening — future arrangement

**Note:** Future plan in Present Continuous = arrangement already confirmed, not just an intention. For intentions use going to.

**Takeaway:** Present Continuous = happening now + temporary + future planned (100% confirmed)

## 3. Present Perfect

**Used for:** experiences, recent events with present result, unfinished actions

```

I / You / We / They + have + past participle
He / She / It + has + past participle

```

**3 situations:**

**Situation 1 — Experience (no specific time)**
Something done or not done in life — exact time doesn't matter.
```

I have visited Mumbai
I have never eaten sushi
Have you ever used Next.js?

```

**Situation 2 — Recent event with present result**
Past action caused a present state — result visible NOW.
```

I have just deployed the frontend app ← app is live now
She has left the office ← she is gone now
I have eaten already ← I am full now

```

**Situation 3 — Unfinished action (for/since)**
Started in past, still continuing now.
```

I have worked here for 3 years ← still working
I have known him since 2019 ← still know him

```

**for vs since:**
- `for` + duration — for 3 years, for a long time
- `since` + starting point — since 2022, since morning

**Golden rule — no specific time:**
```

I visited Mumbai last year ✅ — Simple Past with specific time
I have visited Mumbai ✅ — Present Perfect with no specific time
I have visited Mumbai last year ❌ — wrong

```

**My sentences:**
1. I have just deployed the frontend app
2. I have worked for an MNC
3. I have used all MERN stack technologies
4. I have left the office
5. I have worked there for 3 years

**Takeaway:** Present Perfect = experience + recent result still relevant now + unfinished action

## 4. Present Perfect Continuous

**Used for:** action started in past, still ongoing, result still pending

```

I / You / We / They + have been + verb(ing)
He / She / It + has been + verb(ing)

```

**My rule:**
- Completed action = Present Perfect
- Pending action = Present Perfect Continuous

**My sentences:**
1. I have been chatting with Claude for the past 30 mins
2. I have been learning English for a month
3. I have been living in Hyderabad since 2023

**Takeaway:** Present Perfect Continuous = started in past + still ongoing + result pending
```
