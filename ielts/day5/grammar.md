# All 12 Tenses — Master Reference

---

## 1. Simple Present

**My hack:** Facts, routine, schedule

**My examples:**

1. I work from home
2. I eat dates and figs every morning
3. I drop my wife off at Afzal Gunj every morning
4. The sun rises in the east
5. The flight arrives at 8:00 AM
6. The office closes at 6:30 PM

---

## 2. Present Continuous

**My hack:** Now + temporary + confirmed future (100%)

**My examples:**

1. I am working on Payload CMS
2. I am working with a contractor for 6 months
3. I am visiting Bangalore next week
4. I am using Infinix 30 Pro until I get Google Pixel
5. I am meeting her in the evening

---

## 3. Present Perfect

**My hack:** Experience + action whose impact can be felt directly or indirectly

**My examples:**

1. I have worked on D3
2. I have fixed all the sprint bugs
3. I have booked tickets for Terminator
4. I have left the office
5. I have stopped using my mobile phone

---

## 4. Present Perfect Continuous

**My hack:** Ongoing/unfinished action — emphasising duration

**My examples:**

1. I have been learning English since yesterday
2. I have been working with Lifemine Solutions for 6 months
3. I have been writing homework for the past 1 hour
4. I have been looking for a new job for a year
5. I have been improving my prompts since January

---

## 5. Simple Past

**My hack:** Completed action with time — direct or indirect

**My examples:**

1. I learnt to code in JS during my college days
2. I visited Jordan in 2008
3. I interviewed him while I was working with Sitel
4. I gave an interview yesterday
5. I bunked classes in 9th standard
6. I worked in Dubai in 2012

---

## 6. Past Continuous

**My hack:** Action in past + time OR interrupting event

**My examples:**

1. I was sleeping when the power went off
2. I was praying when you called
3. I was giving an interview when my boss called me
4. I was living happily before COVID
5. I was having a good time with her before I recalled she ditched me in 2019
6. I interviewed him while I was working with Sitel

---

## 7. Past Perfect

**My hack:** Did this FIRST — then something else happened in the past

**My examples:**

1. I had dated her before I got married
2. I had worked on the issues before the meeting
3. I had learnt multiple languages before AI made its entry
4. I had moved to NY before the 9/11 attack
5. I had watched this movie in HD before I saw it in cinema yesterday
6. I had interviewed him before I became manager
7. I had been giving interview when they hit me

---

## 8. Past Perfect Continuous

**My hack:** Duration before a past event — always needs for/since

**My examples:**

1. I had been dating her for 3 years before we got married
2. I had been learning engineering for 7 years before I got a job in 2017
3. I had been hunting for a job for 2 months before I got a job as a consultant

---

## 9. Future Simple

**My hack:** Prediction + Promise + Offer/Help

**My examples:**

1. I will complete my task by evening
2. He will get at least 1 backlog
3. Spyware will pose security issues in China
4. The system will crash if the traffic increases
5. The government will waive off taxes for farmers

---

## 10. Future Continuous

**My hack:** Snapshot of future — in progress at a specific future moment

**My examples:**

1. I will be attending the function
2. I will be going home this weekend
3. I will be looking for new opportunities
4. I will be learning JS tonight

---

## 11. Future Perfect

**My hack:** Finished by then — completed before a specific future time

**Signal word:** by / by the time — not "until"

**My examples:**

1. Our website will have gone live by May
2. I will have done my homework by evening
3. I will have slept before 9
4. I will have read the entire book before the match starts
5. I will have learnt 5 new technologies by the time you finish engineering

---

## 12. Future Perfect Continuous

**My hack:** Still in progress by then — always needs duration (for/since)

**My examples:**

1. I will have been working for 5 hours before the meeting starts
2. She will have been teaching for 10 years before she gets married

## Signal Words — Quick Reference

| Tense                      | Signal Words                                    |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Simple Present             | every day, always, usually, at 9am              |
| Present Continuous         | now, currently, this week, tomorrow (confirmed) |
| Present Perfect            | ever, never, just, already, for, since          |
| Present Perfect Continuous | for, since, how long                            |
| Simple Past                | yesterday, last week, in 2019, during, ago      |
| Past Continuous            | when, while, at that time                       |
| Past Perfect               | before, after, by the time, already             |
| Past Perfect Continuous    | for, since + before past event                  |
| Future Simple              | tomorrow, next week, I think, probably          |
| Future Continuous          | at this time tomorrow, this time next week      |
| Future Perfect             | by Friday, by the time, before                  |
| Future Perfect Continuous  | by + time + for/since                           |

---

## Common mistakes to avoid

- _"a year"_ not _"an year"_
- _"gone"_ not _"went"_ with will have
- _"by/by the time"_ not _"until"_ for Future Perfect
- _"look for"_ not _"look"_ when searching
- _"hunt for a job"_ not _"hunt job"_
- _"an interview"_ not _"a interview"_
- Experience = Present Perfect only — never Continuous
- Past/Future Perfect Continuous always needs duration — for/since
- Past Continuous never stands alone — needs time or event
